## 2024-06-11 - AVR Microcontroller Optimization (Float vs Int)
**Learning:** In Arduino Nano (8-bit AVR microcontrollers like the ATmega328P) and similar low-end embedded environments without a hardware FPU (Floating Point Unit), any math involving `float` variables must be computed in software. This drastically inflates CPU time (taking tens to hundreds of instructions) and significantly increases binary size. Standard smoothing algorithms, like an exponential filter (`val += 0.3 * (raw - val)`), often unintentionally introduce floating-point math inside hot `loop()` structures, harming performance.
**Action:** Always replace fractional coefficient multiplications with their integer approximations (e.g., `(diff * 3) / 10` or a bitshift like `(diff * 3) >> 3` to approximate 0.375) when performing digital filtering on AVR microcontrollers.
